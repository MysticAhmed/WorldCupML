Pipeline

1. Data Loading — Load World Cup matches, 49k international results, and player records. Normalize team names (e.g. "West Germany" → "Germany").
2. Feature Engineering — Compute ELO ratings from full international history, rolling team stats, recent form, head-to-head records, and player quality metrics.
3. Temporal Train/Test Split — Train on 1930-2017 (836 matches), test on 2018-2022 (128 matches). No data leakage — the model never sees future results.
4. XGBoost Training — Gradient boosted trees with RandomizedSearchCV (40 hyperparameter combos), balanced class weights for draw underrepresentation.
5. Prediction — For each match, outputs probabilities for Home Win, Draw, and Away Win.
6. Tournament Simulation — 1,000 Monte Carlo simulations of the full 2026 World Cup bracket to estimate each team's win probability.

Context: Is ~48% Good?

Soccer is one of the hardest sports to predict due to low scoring and frequent upsets. Random guessing across 3 outcomes gives 33%. Professional betting markets typically achieve 50-55%. Our model at 48.4% is competitive, especially considering it correctly predicted both the 2018 and 2022 World Cup winners on completely unseen data.

How the Bracket Works

The 2026 World Cup uses a 48-team format with 12 groups of 4. After the group stage, the top 2 from each group plus the 8 best third-place teams advance to a Round of 32. Our simulator runs 1,000 full tournaments. In each run, every match outcome is sampled from the model's predicted probabilities rather than just picking the favorite. This means upsets happen naturally — just like in real tournaments. The win probabilities shown earlier represent how often each team won across all 1,000 simulations.

Features & Datasets

Datasets Used

📊

WorldCupMatches.csv 964 matches

All FIFA World Cup matches from 1930-2022. Primary training and test data with scores, stages, venues.

🌍

results.csv 49,287 matches

Complete international match history from 1872-2026. Used to calculate ELO ratings — the single most important feature.

👤

WorldCupPlayers.csv 9,069 records

Individual player data from World Cup matches. Aggregated into squad quality metrics per team.

🔄

former_names.csv 36 mappings

Maps historical team names to current names (e.g. "Soviet Union" → "Russia", "West Germany" → "Germany").

28 Engineered Features

Each match is represented by these features, computed from historical data available before the match date.

📈
ELO Ratings (3)
Team ELO, opponent ELO, and ELO difference — computed from 49k international matches
📊
Rolling Stats (12)
Win/draw/loss rates, goals scored/conceded, goal difference — for both teams
🔥
Recent Form (4)
Win rate and goal difference over last 20 matches — captures momentum
⚔️
Head-to-Head (3)
Historical win rate, draw rate, and goal difference between the two teams
👥
Player Quality (2)
Average goal events per player for each team's World Cup squad
🏟️
Match Context (4)
Tournament stage, match importance weight, neutral venue flag, year
